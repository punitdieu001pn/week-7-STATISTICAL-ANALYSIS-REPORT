HYPOTHESIS TEST RESULTS

---------------------------------------

1. Churn vs Monthly Charges
Test Used: Independent T-Test

Result:
The p-value is less than 0.05.

Conclusion:
We reject the null hypothesis.
There is a significant relationship between monthly charges and customer churn.
Customers with higher monthly charges are more likely to churn.

---------------------------------------

2. Senior Citizen vs Monthly Charges
Test Used: Independent T-Test

Result:
The p-value is less than 0.05.

Conclusion:
We reject the null hypothesis.
There is a significant difference in monthly charges between senior and non-senior customers.

---------------------------------------

3. Contract Type vs Monthly Charges
Test Used: One-Way ANOVA

Result:
The p-value is greater than 0.05.

Conclusion:
We fail to reject the null hypothesis.
There is no significant difference in monthly charges across different contract types.

---------------------------------------

FINAL INTERPRETATION:

- Monthly charges play an important role in customer churn.
- Senior citizens have different spending behavior.
- Contract type does not significantly affect monthly charges.
